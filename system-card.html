System card

What's inside, what we measured, and — the boring but honest part — what we tested and did not choose. SegSmart is a local-first SME customer segmentation + campaign tool; every decision below is biased toward staying lightweight and keeping data on the owner's machine.

v0.1.0-alpha · revenue figures from UCI Online Retail (4,338 customers) and a 20-month synthetic CZ eshop · all numbers reproducible from the repo.

Decisions & what we tested

AreaShipped choiceAlso testedWhy not chosen
Segmentation RFM quantile rules + KMeans cross-check 2D-attention encoder (SAINT-lite, self-supervised) Its clusters were worse (silhouette 0.18 vs 0.25) and it needs PyTorch. RFM is already a clean feature space — nothing left for a net to learn.
Lookalikes / prospects Cosine k-NN on the scaled RFM features Embeddings from the same attention encoder Lookalike quality was lower (Champion-purity 0.74 vs 0.78) for a large dependency. Plain geometry wins; runs in milliseconds.
Forecasting (not shipped yet) ETS / Holt-Winters (statsmodels) if/when added Chronos-Bolt zero-shot (time-series foundation model) Result split by data: Chronos won on clean synthetic (MASE 0.694 vs 0.761), ETS won on the real, spiky series (1.256 vs 1.430). Chronos needs torch + transformers (which clashes with our research env) + a model download; ETS is milliseconds. Kept as a documented option for clean / never-seen-before series.
Campaign copy Local LLM via Ollama (gemma fast / qwen quality) gemma4:e4b · gemma4:26b · qwen3.6:35b · Nemotron-49B 26B too slow on CPU; Nemotron / a qwen-MoE build produced gibberish or errored on Czech. Impact numbers are deterministic, never written by the model.
Category enrichment Catalog file → local LLM → heuristic fallback LLM-only categorisation Catalog-first is exact and offline-safe; the LLM only fills genuine gaps.
Ingestion Encoding/delimiter sniffer + canonical loader — Swallows messy real exports (cp1250, semicolons, xlsx, missing columns) without a cloud parser.

Deliberately not in the product

  • Deep learning & foundation models — tested (attention for segments, Chronos for forecasts); they didn't beat simple methods enough to justify the weight at SME scale.
  • PyTorch / transformers as runtime deps — they're heavy and version-fragile (the Chronos test alone forced a transformers downgrade). The product stays on pandas/sklearn/statsmodels.
  • Any cloud call — no SaaS inference, no telemetry. The customer database never leaves the machine; that's the moat, not a limitation.

How it's validated

  • Segmentation quality: KMeans silhouette + RFM↔KMeans agreement (adjusted Rand index), shown on the dashboard.
  • Honesty warnings: the tool flags when the data can't support a conclusion (short window, too few customers, weak structure) instead of pretending.
  • Executable policies: 198 automated tests, including a no-PII guard (hashed name denylist), a bilingual-UI contract, and a docs-sync rule — the rules are tests, not just prose.
Methodology note: the experiments above (attention encoder, forecasting bake-off) live on dedicated branches in the repository, with the scripts that produce every number here. We keep negative results on purpose.
